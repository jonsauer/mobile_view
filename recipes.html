Double Chocolate Cookies

Origin: Michigan Source: Family Recipe Category: Dessert

My daughter learned to make these cookies at a baking camp at Zingermanns and has tweaked the recipe to fit the taste buds of her siblings. They are extremely sugary so the salt helps to balance it. Note, these cookies are best eaten very quickly.

Recipe Ingredients

  • Unsalted butter
  • Granulated Sugar
  • Packed light or dark brown sugar
  • Large egg
  • Pure vanilla extract
  • Semi-sweet chocolate chunks (melted)
  • All-purpose flour
  • Natural unsweetened cocoa powder
  • Baking soda
  • Salt
  • Semi-sweet chocolate chunks

Recipe Steps

  1. 1. In a mixing bowl cream together the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar
  2. 2. Add the egg and vanilla extract and beat well
  3. 3. Add the melted chocolate
  4. 4. In a separate bowl combine the flour, baking soda, cocoa powder and salt
  5. 5. Combine the wet and dry ingredients
  6. 6. Add the unmelted chocolate chunks.
  7. 7. Form 15 cookies and place on a baking sheet.
  8. 8. Cook for 12 to 13 minutes at 350 degrees.

Sushi Bake

Origin: Asian Source: Own recipe Category: Main dish

Do you want easy, quick way to enjoy sushi without having to do all of the work? Try to make our sushi bake, which takes 10 minutes to assemble and 20 minutes to bake in the oven. Our recipe feeds 8 people.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 1 avocado
  • 1/2 cup soon sauce
  • 2 cups of rice
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar
  • 3 tablespoons of mayo
  • 1 8 oz package of imitation crab
  • 1 medium piece of salmon
  • 1 cucumber
  • 3 tablespoons of sriracha

Recipe Steps

  1. 1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees
  2. 2. Make rice. Mix rice vinegar into rice after it is cooked
  3. 3. Lay out all of the rice at the bottom of a large baking dish
  4. 4. Lay out the imitation crab and cucumber on top of the rice
  5. 5. Mix the imitation crab and 2 TBSP mayo into a crab salad
  6. 6. Smear the crab salad onto the next layer
  7. 7. Slice the avocado and lay those on the top of the dish
  8. 8. Pour the rest of the mayo and sriracha on top
  9. 9. Bake for 20 minutes
  10. 10. Enjoy!

Dead Simple Fried Rice

Origin: American/East Asian Source: Self Category: Main Dish

This Dead Simple Fried Rice recipe is an incredibly modular and tasty dish. At its core, it's just rice, vegetables, protein, and oil. As such, the dish can be modified to serve as many or as few people as needed with a wide variety of food preferences. Experimentation with what specific ingredients you enjoy the most will unlock the full potential of this dish.

Recipe Ingredients

  • Long-grain white rice
  • Frozen Vegetables
  • Eggs
  • Large egg
  • (optional) Milk
  • Oil of Choice
  • Salt
  • Soy Sauce
  • (optional) Additional Sauce Curry Sauce/Paste, Spicy Teriyaki Sauce, and Sweet Chili Sauce are great options
  • (optional) Additional Pre-cooked/Fast-cooking Protein of Choice Nuts, sausage, tofu, and bacon are great options
  • (optional) Additional Fresh Vegetables Green onions, celery, and vidalia onions add great texture and flavor to the dish.

Recipe Steps

  1. 1. In a rice cooker, add half a cup of rice, a cup of water, a touch of oil, and a pinch of salt. Cook the rice. Tip: if there are tough frozen veggies, such as broccoli, you might want to add the frozen veggies to the integrated steamer basket.
  2. 2. On high medium heat on your stovetop, in a frying pan, add your chosen oil. Add some milk if desired. Then, scramble the eggs.
  3. 3. Before the eggs are fully done, add the frozen vegetables. Add fresh vegetables when required so they are fully cooked by the end.
  4. 4. (Optional) Add the additional protein when required so they are fully cooked by the end.
  5. 5. Once the rice is done, add it to the pan. Poor your desired amount of Soy Sauce. Stir well.
  6. 6. (Optional) After that, add your desired amount of additional sauce. Stir well.
  7. 7. Let the fried rice cook until it is no longer visibly oily and the dish is nice and hot, with the vegetables fully cooked.
  8. 8. Plate the fried rice. Enjoy!

White Sauce Pasta

Origin: France/Italy Source: Own Recipe Category: Main Dish

White sauce pasta is a creamy and savory dish that can be enjoyed for either lunch or dinner. Inspired by a fusion of Italian and French cuisine, the rough texture of rigatoni pasta allows for heavier sauces to be picked up, including the flavor profile of this delicious sauce. This union of two cultures allows for the perfect balance between each component of the recipe, creating a fulfilling entree that every individual can enjoy.

Recipe Ingredients

  • Olive Oil
  • Butter
  • Diced Onion
  • Diced Garlic
  • Diced Bell Pepper
  • Rigatoni Pasta
  • Heavy Whipping Cream
  • Milk
  • Shredded Parmesan Cheese
  • Salt
  • Ground Pepper
  • Garlic Powder
  • Onion Powder
  • Paprika
  • Oregano Leaves
  • Bay Leaves
  • Parsley Leaves

Recipe Steps

  1. 1. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Once the water begins to bubble, add in the pasta, salt, and a drizzle of oil.
  2. 2. While the pasta is cooking, heat oil and butter in a saucepan.
  3. 3. Once the butter has melted, add in diced onions and garlic. Saut√© them until golden brown or aromatic.
  4. 4. Stir in the bell pepper until slightly softened.
  5. 5. Pour in the heavy cream, milk, salt, ground pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, oregano leaves, and bay leaves into the saucepan. Mix well and allow the mixture to simmer. Adjust the seasoning to your taste.
  6. 6. Add the shredded parmesan cheese and stir until melted.
  7. 7. Throw in pasta and mix until the pasta is coated with the creamy sauce.
  8. 8. Garnish with parsley leaves, and enjoy.
